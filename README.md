## My learning approach

Four rules I follow every session:

1. **Learn from every alert** — understand WHY a detection rule triggered, not just that it did
2. **Think like the attacker** — ask what the attacker's purpose is before analyzing the defense
3. **Verify everything** — never assume, always validate in logs
4. **Get involved in real incidents** — labs teach technique, incidents teach judgment

## Why OT/ICS?

Critical infrastructure (energy, water, transport, industry) is the fastest-growing
attack surface in cybersecurity. NIS2 in France creates strong regulatory demand
for OT-aware SOC analysts at companies like EDF, SNCF, Veolia, Schneider Electric.

My angle: SOC L1 generalist skills + OT protocol awareness (Modbus, OPC UA,
Purdue model) + GRFICS home lab = differentiated profile for NIS2-driven companies.
