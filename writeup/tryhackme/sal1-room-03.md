The Humans aspect:

Humans are the weakest threats for cyber security. 
Therefore the attackers target the human weaknesses by aim Trust or emotional aspect.
Attackers try to manipulate the humans who could give them the access to what they want (bank account, credentials, authorisation, database). They use social engineering to manipulate humans, it consist by seeking and use accurate informations on the target but also on who they impersonate (you make yourself like you are someone else for instance a company who sell a product or a CEO) as much as possible to make their attacks more trustable. 
Then they delivers their attacks, they are severals methods like the phishing (it is a fake message using informations who seems right) it’s the most common threat who use social engineering, it could be a response of a job interview where they ask you for your number of phone or your boss who ask for your ID cards to make you a new badge because the company change their location so you need to renew your badge. However they are also the insider threat (a person from your own company who’s malicious but you don’t know and manipulate you to give your access), the usb drop campaigns someone who plug a usb who contains a malware, physical attacks (the attackers come directly at the location and manipulate the gatekeepers « in real life » to get the access (the key) and get what they want).

A computer network is a basement with differents floors with huge wall and gate and to pass to another room or go inside the room you need to have the access (by using password, card, credentials and right). Attackers use methods to create breach into the walls or manipulate to get access of the gate

Nevertheless we can increase the security of our wall and our gate.
By using Mitigation and Detection.

Mitigation is the measure we put in place to detect and manage a threat.

It is key for SOC because with a smart mitigation they are less issues, threats are treated efficiently. 
We can involve the systems of mitigation by training our employee for example we can build a system who one times randomly during the week  to test whiteout knowing the moment, a phishing mail for exemple then we give them a review (a score) on how they behave, to help employees to create smart habits and become more aware on cyberthreat.
In addition they are solutions like EDR (endpoint detection respond) it is an antivirus based on behaviours, anti phishing, XDR (a smarter EDR).


If the Mitigation is passed it’s at this moment SOC take the handle on the incident to analyse and give a respond.
The SOC should verify and if the incident get a truly malicious signs (by recognition of patterns) they should have zero trust and directly block.
