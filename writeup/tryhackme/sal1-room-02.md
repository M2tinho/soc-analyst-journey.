SAL1 — Room 02 : SOC Analyst

The analyst mindset

The biggest gap between junior and senior analysts isn't technical knowledge. It's how they approach an alert. Seniors have internalized a set of habits that 

turn raw data into reliable conclusions.


My four rules (learned from this room)

1. Learn from every alert

Don't just close the ticket. Ask: why did this rule trigger? Understanding the detection logic behind an alert sharpens your ability to spot gaps and reduce false positives over time.

2. Think like the attacker

Before analyzing the defense, ask: what was the attacker trying to achieve? Purpose-first thinking changes what you look for in logs.

3. Verify everything — never assume

An alert is a hypothesis, not a fact. Always validate in logs. Use AI for large-scale triage, but keep the final judgment yours — AI reduces noise, human logic confirms intent.

4. Get involved in real incidents

Labs teach you the technique. Real incidents teach you judgment. Ask senior analysts questions during active incidents. That exposure compounds faster than any course.

Why this matters at L1

L1 analysts see high alert volume. The temptation is to process tickets fast and move on. The analysts who grow quickly are the ones who treat each alert as a micro-learning opportunity — even when the answer is "false positive, no action needed."

Key takeaway

Consistency beats intensity. One hour of deliberate, questioning analysis per day compounds into strong pattern recognition within months. Speed comes after understanding — not before.
